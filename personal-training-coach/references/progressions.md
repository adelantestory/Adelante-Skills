# Progressions Reference

## 1RM Calculation — Epley Formula

```
Estimated 1RM = Weight × (1 + Reps / 30)
```

**Example:** 185 lb × 6 reps → 185 × (1 + 6/30) = 185 × 1.2 = **222 lb estimated 1RM**

Works equally in kg — the formula is unit-agnostic. Simply use kg throughout and your working weights will be in kg.

---

## Working Weight Calculation

```
Hypertrophy working weight = 1RM × 0.70 to 0.75
Strength working weight    = 1RM × 0.80 to 0.85
Definition working weight  = 1RM × 0.60 to 0.70
```

Round to the nearest practical increment — 5 lbs, 2.5 kg, or whatever your smallest available plate allows.

---

## Rep Test Protocol

1. Warm up progressively — a light set of 10 reps, then a moderate set of 5 reps with more weight, then load the bar for your working test set. You are warming up by feel, not by percentages — you don't yet know your 1RM.
2. Choose a weight you expect to lift for 4–8 hard reps based on your best estimate
3. Lift until you have exactly 1 rep clearly left in the tank — do not go to complete failure
4. Apply Epley formula to get your estimated 1RM
5. Calculate working weights from that 1RM

**Testing sequencing:** No more than 2–3 major lifts per session to avoid fatigue affecting results. Spread baseline testing across the first week of training.

## Progressive Overload Rules By Goal

### Hypertrophy (8–12 rep range)
When all sets hit the **top of the rep range** with 1–2 reps left in tank → add 5 lbs next session
- Upper body lifts: +5 lbs
- Lower body lifts: +5–10 lbs

### Definition / Recomposition (12–15 rep range)
When all sets hit 15 reps comfortably with 2+ reps in tank → add 5 lbs next session

### Strength (3–6 rep range)
When all prescribed sets are completed cleanly with 1 rep in tank → add 5 lbs next session

### Endurance
When prescribed distance or duration feels clearly manageable → increase volume by 10% next session

### Bodyweight Movements
When hitting the rep ceiling consistently across all sets:
1. Add load (weighted vest, DB, resistance band)
2. Or progress to a harder variation
3. Or reduce rest periods

## Stall Diagnosis Protocol

When a lift stalls (no progress for 2+ consecutive sessions at same weight):

**Step 1 — Check the basics:**
- Sleep: Less than 7 hours consistently? Recovery is compromised.
- Nutrition: Is protein target being hit? Is caloric surplus maintained?
- Stress: High stress periods suppress training adaptation.
- Volume: Too many sessions without adequate rest?

**Step 2 — Assess the pattern:**
- Stalling on all lifts simultaneously → likely a nutrition or recovery issue
- Stalling on one specific lift → likely a technique or muscle weakness issue
- Stalling after rapid initial progress → normal adaptation — reduce weight 10%, rebuild

**Step 3 — Apply the appropriate fix:**
- Sleep/nutrition issue → address the underlying cause, maintain current weights
- Single lift technique → film the lift, identify the sticking point, address weakness
- Normal adaptation plateau → deload week (50–60% intensity), then restart with slight weight reduction

## Goal-Specific Rebaseline Protocols

### Muscle Building / Body Recomposition

**Collect:**
- Morning body weight
- Body measurements: Chest · Right shoulder · Waist · Hips · Right bicep · Right quad · Right calf
- Strength retest on all major lifts

**Measurement protocol:** Morning, before eating, unflexed, same tape position every time.

**Assessment scenarios:**

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Weight up + waist flat or down | Lean muscle gain — stay the course |
| Weight up + waist up 0.5" | Surplus slightly aggressive — reduce carbs 150 cal |
| Weight up + waist up 1"+ | Excessive surplus — reduce carbs 300 cal |
| Weight flat + measurements improving | Recomposition working — stay the course |
| Strength up all lifts | Program working — progress weights |
| Strength stalled | Check sleep, nutrition, volume — see stall diagnosis |
| No change anywhere after 8 weeks | Reassess goal alignment and program design |

---

### Weight Loss

**Collect:**
- Morning body weight
- Waist and hip measurements — most meaningful for fat loss progress
- Optional: Chest and shoulder if upper body composition is important to the person

**Do not:** Retest strength maxes unless specifically requested — strength testing under caloric deficit is often demoralizing and less informative than scale and tape data.

**Assessment scenarios:**

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Weight down + waist down | Fat loss working — maintain deficit |
| Weight down + strength maintained | Excellent — muscle preserved, stay the course |
| Weight down + strength dropping significantly | Losing muscle — increase protein, reduce deficit slightly |
| Weight stalled 2+ weeks | Reduce calories 150–200 (carbs first) or increase activity |
| Losing faster than 1 lb/week | Increase protein, consider slight calorie increase |
| Energy very low, fatigue | Increase calories slightly, check protein timing |

---

### Strength Building

**Collect:**
- Morning body weight
- Strength retest on all major compound lifts
- Optional body measurements only if person is interested

**Assessment scenarios:**

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| 1RM up 5–10% | Excellent progress — continue program |
| 1RM up 1–5% | Solid progress — stay the course |
| 1RM flat | Investigate — see stall diagnosis above |
| 1RM down 1–5% | Check recovery and nutrition |
| 1RM down 5%+ | Review program design and overall health |

---

### Endurance

**Collect:**
- Morning body weight
- Performance retest: Same benchmark as initial baseline (time trial, distance, pace)
- Rate of perceived exertion at the same effort level as last rebaseline

**Do not:** Collect body measurements or test strength maxes unless the person specifically requests it.

**Assessment scenarios:**

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Pace improved or distance increased | Program working — progress volume or intensity |
| Same performance at lower perceived effort | Fitness improving — ready to increase challenge |
| Performance flat after 8 weeks | Review training zones, recovery, and nutrition |
| Performance regressed | Check for overtraining, illness, or life stress |

---

### Mobility and Health

**Collect:**
- Morning body weight (informational)
- Movement reassessment: Revisit the specific limitations identified at intake
- Functional markers: What can they do now that they couldn't before?
- Subjective report: Pain levels, daily energy, sleep quality

**Do not:** Test 1RMs, collect body measurements, or run performance benchmarks unless specifically relevant.

**Assessment scenarios:**

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Range of motion improved | Program working — progress to next movement challenge |
| Pain reduced or eliminated | Major win — maintain and build on what's working |
| Functional markers improving | Stay the course |
| No change after 8 weeks | Reassess movement screen — identify what's still limiting |
| Pain increased | Stop the aggravating movements immediately — reassess |

---

### General Fitness

**Collect:**
- Morning body weight
- Waist measurement
- One or two performance markers relevant to their activities
- Subjective assessment: Energy levels, sleep, how they feel day to day

**Assessment scenarios:**

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Feeling better, more energy | Program working — stay the course |
| Performance markers improving | Progress challenge appropriately |
| Weight trending toward goal | Nutrition working |
| No change after 8 weeks | Identify the highest-leverage change and make it |

## Rebaseline Strength Assessment (Strength and Muscle Goals)

Compare current 1RM estimate to previous rebaseline:

| Change | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| +5–10% | Excellent progress — program working well |
| +1–5% | Solid progress — stay the course |
| 0% (flat) | Investigate — see stall diagnosis |
| −1 to −5% | Slight regression — check recovery and nutrition |
| −5%+ | Significant regression — review program design and health |
